Kheoni Wildlife Sanctuary

Nestled in the heart of Madhya Pradesh, Kheoni Wildlife Sanctuary spans 132 square kilometers of pristine wilderness across Dewas and Sehore districts. This hidden gem is connected to Ratapani Tiger Reserve through natural corridors, creating a vital lifeline for wildlife movement in Central India.

The sanctuary's dry deciduous forests are dominated by teak, tendu, and bamboo, creating the perfect habitat for a rich diversity of flora and fauna. As you venture through Kheoni's untamed landscapes, you'll discover why this protected area has become one of central India's most promising wildlife destinations.

Experience Kheoni

Remarkable Biodiversity

Big Cats & Predators

Tiger

Migrated from Ratapani, these magnificent cats have successfully colonized Kheoni, establishing it as an emerging tiger territory.

Leopard

Present in significant numbers, these elusive and adaptable big cats can be spotted in the sanctuary's dense forest areas.

Striped Hyena

These nocturnal scavengers play a vital role in the ecosystem by cleaning up carcasses and maintaining forest health.

Herbivores

Chital (Spotted Deer)

The most commonly sighted deer species in Kheoni, recognizable by their distinctive white spots and elegant antlers.

Nilgai

India's largest antelope species, these majestic creatures can be spotted in Kheoni's grasslands and open woodlands.

Blackbuck

Known for their distinctive spiral horns and contrasting black and white coloration, these swift antelopes are a joy to observe.

Avian Diversity

A 2018 survey identified approximately 125 bird species in Kheoni, making it a paradise for birdwatchers.

Indian Paradise Flycatcher

The state bird of Madhya Pradesh, known for its long, streaming white tail feathers that flow behind it in flight.

Plum-headed Parakeet

These colorful birds add vibrant splashes of green and purple to Kheoni's canopy, often seen in small flocks.

Black Drongo

Known for its aggressive behavior, this glossy black bird with a distinctive forked tail is a skilled aerial hunter.

Forest Ecosystem

Kheoni's dry deciduous forests represent one of central India's most important ecosystems. Dominated by valuable timber species like teak, the sanctuary's vegetation also includes tendu (Indian ebony) and bamboo groves that provide crucial habitat for wildlife.
